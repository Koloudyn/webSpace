The observable universe contains an estimated 10 22 to 10 24 stars. Only about 4,000 of these stars are visible to the naked eye, all within the Milky Way galaxy.

Astronomers often group stars by their mass:

  • Very low mass stars, with masses below 0.5 M☉.
  • Low mass stars (including the Sun), with a mass between 0.5 M☉ and ~2.25 M☉.
  • Intermediate-mass stars, between ~2.25 M☉ and ~8 M☉.
  • Massive stars generally have a minimum mass of ~8 M☉.
